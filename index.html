04The portfolio — assets to build

Hiring managers in writing / research / content / PMM roles hire on artifacts, not credentials. They want process, not pixels — which means Figma polish matters far less than you fear. Build these in order; each is a weekend-sized deliverable.

  • 1 · Positioning one-liner + retitled identity. Lead with a hybrid title recruiters search for — "UX Writer & Content Designer." One sentence: "I turn messy product problems into clear words and tested decisions — including with AI tools that let me prototype faster than most."
  • 2 · A 1-page portfolio site. Notion public page, Framer, or Read.cv — zero design skill needed. Hero line → 3 case studies → about → contact. (This site can be that home.)
  • 3a · Case study — UX writing. Audit a real app's bad copy, redesign one flow's content (onboarding, empty states, errors, paywall). Show before/after. Needs only a doc + screenshots.
  • 3b · Case study — research. Run one small real study: recruit 5 people, test a product/prototype, synthesize into an affinity map + 3 findings + recommendations. Tools: Google Meet, FigJam free, a Maze test.
  • 3c · Case study — your AI edge. Document one AI no-code build as a product case study: problem, user, what you built, what you'd test next. This separates you from every other junior applicant.
  • 4 · Messaging sample pack (for PMM / content). A mock launch: positioning statement, 3 value props, a landing hero, 2 social posts, one email. One page.
  • 5 · Insights deck (for research / CX / strategy). A question, a method, 3 slides of findings, a recommendation. Reuse from 3b.
  • 6 · Tuned CV + LinkedIn. Mirror the keywords of your target roles. Make AI-tool fluency a named skill block, not a footnote.

How to brainstorm work when you have no client projects

  • Teardowns / redesigns — pick a product with a visible flaw and fix it. Unlimited supply, shows judgment.
  • Speculative projects, clearly labeled — accepted for juniors as long as the process is real.
  • Volunteer / nonprofit work — a real stakeholder = a stronger story.
  • "Rewrite of the week" — post one microcopy teardown weekly. Doubles as portfolio and recruiter visibility.
  • Reframe your AI builds — each one already is a case study; you just haven't written the narrative.
  • 3 strong case studies beats 8 thin ones. Depth signals seniority.

05Hard skills to learn — ranked by ROI

You don't need to become a Figma expert. You need enough tool fluency to not get filtered out — then lean into words, research, and AI-shipping.

Tier 1 — learn now

High value · low effort
  • Figma — basic literacy only. Open files, comment, navigate, FigJam. A weekend. Don't over-invest.
  • One research tool: Dovetail (synthesis) or Maze (testing). Run one study, name it.
  • AI tooling, made explicit: prompt engineering + an LLM + one no-code builder (Lovable, Bolt, v0). Your edge — name the tools.

Tier 2 — high leverage

For adjacent roles
  • Analytics: GA4, Amplitude, or Mixpanel basics (PMM, CX, product).
  • Notion / Confluence + a content-ops mindset.
  • SEO + content marketing — HubSpot Academy free certs. Fast and resume-legible.

Tier 3 — situational

Role-dependent
  • SQL basics — big differentiator for research/analyst roles.
  • Hebrew↔English UX writing — marketable in this market specifically.
  • Webflow / Framer — if leaning into content + light build.
Don't spend time on: deep visual/UI design, motion, design-systems mastery. That's chasing the gap you already decided not to compete in.

06The forcing function: a 4-week "Ship in Public" sprint

Motivation evaporates without an external deadline. Announce on LinkedIn: "For the next 4 weeks I'm publishing one UX writing/research teardown every Friday." Public commitment + weekly cadence = built-in accountability and recruiter visibility at once.

Week 1
Content teardown. Audit a real app's copy, publish the before/after redesign. → Case study 3a.
Week 2
AI-build case study. Write the narrative around one of your no-code projects. → Case study 3c, your differentiator.
Week 3
Research study. Run the 5-person study, publish the insights deck. → Case study 3b + 5.
Week 4
Messaging pack. Mock launch for a product you love. → Asset 4, aimed at content/PMM roles.
Two more forcing functions to stack: (1) Apply backwards from job descriptions — pull 10 real TLV posts, extract every required tool, build the portfolio piece that proves each. The market becomes your curriculum. (2) Weekly review — book a recurring 20-min slot with someone who gets a deliverable every Friday. External eyes = accountability.

End state after 4 weeks: a full 3-case-study portfolio, 4 posts of recruiter reach, the Tier-1 skills named on your CV, and an income stream running. Then point applications at the top-3 bets.
